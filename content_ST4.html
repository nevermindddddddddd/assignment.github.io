The Importance of Global Partnerships

Addressing global hunger requires coordinated action across sectors, borders, and disciplines. No single organization or government can solve this complex challenge alone. Partnerships bring together diverse expertise, resources, and perspectives to create more effective and sustainable solutions.

The UN Sustainable Development Goal #2: Zero Hunger explicitly recognizes the importance of partnerships, calling for increased investment in rural infrastructure, agricultural research, and international cooperation to end hunger by 2030.

Key Benefits of Partnerships:

  • Pooling of resources and expertise
  • Scaling of successful interventions
  • Knowledge sharing and innovation
  • Coordinated response to complex challenges
  • Increased accountability and transparency

Our Key Partners

The Zero Hunger Initiative collaborates with a diverse range of partners across sectors to maximize our impact. These partnerships enable us to leverage complementary strengths and create comprehensive approaches to food security.

International Organizations

We work closely with UN agencies and international organizations that specialize in food security, agriculture, and nutrition. These partnerships provide access to global resources, expertise, and networks.

Key Partners:

  • Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO) - Collaborating on sustainable agriculture practices and policy development
  • World Food Programme (WFP) - Partnering on emergency food assistance and resilience building
  • UNICEF - Working together on child nutrition and school feeding programs
  • International Fund for Agricultural Development (IFAD) - Cooperating on rural development and smallholder farming initiatives

Governments and Public Sector

Collaboration with national and local governments is essential for creating enabling policy environments, mobilizing resources, and ensuring the sustainability of interventions.

Partnership Focus:

  • Policy development and implementation
  • Integration of food security into national development plans
  • Public investment in agriculture and nutrition
  • Strengthening of public food distribution systems

Private Sector

Businesses bring innovation, efficiency, and resources to the fight against hunger. Our private sector partnerships focus on creating shared value that benefits communities while advancing business objectives.

Collaboration Areas:

  • Sustainable supply chain development
  • Food fortification and nutrition innovation
  • Digital solutions for agriculture
  • Market access for smallholder farmers

Civil Society and NGOs

Civil society organizations provide grassroots connections, community trust, and specialized expertise. These partnerships ensure our work is responsive to local needs and contexts.

Key Contributions:

  • Community mobilization and engagement
  • Local knowledge and contextual understanding
  • Advocacy and accountability
  • Direct implementation of programs

Partnership Success Stories

East Africa: Climate Resilience Alliance

A partnership between our initiative, the FAO, local governments, and private sector companies has helped 250,000 smallholder farmers in Kenya, Tanzania, and Ethiopia adopt climate-resilient agricultural practices.

Key Achievements:

  • 40% increase in crop yields despite climate challenges
  • 30% reduction in post-harvest losses
  • Improved access to markets for 150,000 farmers
  • Significant improvement in household nutrition diversity

This partnership demonstrates how coordinated action across sectors can create sustainable solutions that address multiple dimensions of food security.

South Asia: School Nutrition Partnership

A collaborative effort between our initiative, UNICEF, national governments, and local food producers has established sustainable school feeding programs reaching 1.2 million children across South Asia.

Impact:

  • 15% increase in school attendance
  • Improved nutritional status for participating children
  • Creation of stable markets for 75,000 local smallholder farmers
  • Integration of nutrition education into school curricula

This partnership creates a virtuous cycle: improving child nutrition and education while supporting local food systems and economies.

Get Involved in Our Partnerships

We are always seeking new partners who share our commitment to ending hunger and building sustainable food systems. There are many ways to engage with our partnership network:

Partnership Opportunities

  • Become a Funding Partner: Support our programs financially to help scale proven interventions.
  • Technical Collaboration: Share expertise and technical resources to strengthen our approaches.
  • Implementation Partner: Work with us to implement programs in communities where you operate.
  • Advocacy Partner: Join our efforts to influence policy and raise awareness about hunger issues.
  • Research Partner: Collaborate on research to develop evidence-based solutions to food insecurity.

To explore partnership opportunities, please contact our Partnership Development Team at partnerships@zerohunger.org or complete our Partnership Interest Form.
